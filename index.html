DAISY

Summary:

The Drifting Acoustic Instrumentation SYstem, DAISY, is an instrumentation package developed at the University of Washington for recording sound in high-energy marine environments. These systems are used to study noise propogration from marine energy converters (MECs) to inform regulations for noise emissions from those devices.

My Role:

The DAISYs are a custom system that were designed, manufactured, and assembled by my colleagues and I at the University of Washington. My tasks included: designing custom electronics with multiple sensors and a high-fidelity hydrophone recording system, along with creating the onboard control software and data offload GUI.
